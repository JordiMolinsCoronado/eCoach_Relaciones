# Product Due Diligence Template

## Purpose

This file helps review investment products before any human decision.

The assistant may summarize, compare, identify risks, and generate questions.
The assistant must not recommend the product or say it is suitable.

---

## 1. Product identity

Collect:

- Product name
- ISIN
- Provider / manager
- Domicile
- Currency
- Asset class
- Product type
- Share class
- Accumulating or distributing
- Launch date
- Fund size / AUM
- Benchmark
- Regulatory document available: KID, factsheet, prospectus, annual report

---

## 2. Product summary

Summarize:

- What the product is
- What exposure it gives
- What it tries to achieve
- Whether it is active or passive
- Whether it distributes income or accumulates
- Whether it uses leverage, derivatives, or complex structures
- Whether it is liquid or illiquid

---

## 3. Possible role in portfolio

Describe possible roles only, not recommendations.

Possible roles:

- Cash-like holding
- Short-term parking
- Defensive fixed income
- Income generation
- Diversification
- Equity growth exposure
- Inflation hedge
- Currency exposure
- Alternative exposure
- Satellite allocation
- Core allocation candidate

## 4. Exposure analysis

Clarify:

- Asset class exposure
- Region exposure
- Sector exposure
- Currency exposure
- Credit exposure
- Duration exposure
- Interest-rate sensitivity
- Equity beta
- Concentration
- Counterparty exposure
- Liquidity exposure

## 5. Costs and fees

Collect:

- TER / OCF
- Management fee
- Performance fee
- Entry fee
- Exit fee
- Subscription/redemption costs
- Trading costs
- Bid-ask spread
- Platform custody costs
- Retrocessions
- Clean share class availability
- Currency conversion costs
- Tax/reporting costs

Ask:

- Are there cheaper comparable alternatives?
- Is there a clean share class?
- Are all-in costs transparent?

---

## 6. Liquidity

Clarify:

- Daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, or locked liquidity
- Notice periods
- Redemption gates
- Suspension risk
- Settlement time
- Minimum holding period
- Minimum subscription
- Transferability
- Secondary market availability if applicable

---

## 7. Risk analysis

Identify relevant risks:

- Market risk
- Credit risk
- Duration / interest-rate risk
- Liquidity risk
- Currency risk
- Concentration risk
- Counterparty risk
- Derivative risk
- Leverage risk
- Complexity risk
- Manager risk
- Tracking-error risk
- Benchmark risk
- Inflation risk
- Tax/reporting risk
- Behavioural risk

---

## 8. Performance caveats

When discussing performance, note:

- Past performance does not guarantee future results.
- Recent performance may reflect unusual market conditions.
- Performance should be compared to a relevant benchmark.
- Net performance after fees matters.
- Currency effects may distort returns.
- Risk-adjusted performance matters.
- Drawdowns matter.
- Volatility matters.
- Time period selection can mislead.

---

## 9. Client fit questions

Do not decide fit. Generate questions.

Ask:

- What client objective would this product serve?
- What time horizon is required?
- What losses could the client tolerate?
- Does the client understand the product?
- Does the product match the clientâ€™s liquidity needs?
- Does the product create tax/reporting complexity?
- Does the product duplicate existing exposures?
- Does the product create concentration risk?
- Does the productâ€™s cost need to be justified?
- Is there a simpler comparable alternative?

## 10. Red flags

Flag:

- Product too complex for client knowledge
- Illiquid product for short-term need
- High fees without clear justification
- Currency exposure not understood
- Credit or duration risk hidden by â€œconservativeâ€ label
- Structured product payoff not understood
- Leverage or derivatives not understood
- Poor tax reporting
- High concentration
- Provider conflict of interest
- Product pushed before client profile is clear
- Missing KID/factsheet/prospectus
- Performance presented without risk context

---

## 11. Questions for provider or platform

Ask:

- What is the exact share class?
- What are the all-in costs?
- Is there a clean share class?
- What liquidity terms apply?
- What risks are most material?
- What benchmark is used?
- How has the product behaved in stress periods?
- What currency exposure exists?
- Are derivatives or leverage used?
- Are there redemption restrictions?
- What Spanish tax-reporting support exists?
- What documents are available?
