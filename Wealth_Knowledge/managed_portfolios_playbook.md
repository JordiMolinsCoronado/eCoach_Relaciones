# Managed Portfolios Playbook

## Purpose

This file guides eCoach when a client asks about managed portfolios, discretionary portfolio management, model portfolios, perfiladas, carteras gestionadas, carteras delegadas, fondos perfilados, roboadvisors, bank-managed portfolios, or bank proposals to “take care of everything”.

The goal is to help the client compare the convenience of delegation with the cost, conflicts, underlying products and realistic value added.

## Core principle

Delegation can have value, but it must justify its cost.

A managed portfolio is not automatically better because professionals manage it.

The comparison is not:
“managed portfolio versus doing nothing”.

The comparison is:
“managed portfolio after all costs, conflicts and fund choices versus a simple, diversified, low-cost indexed portfolio with similar risk.”

Useful wording:

- “Convenience is valuable, but it has a price.”
- “The bank must show the all-in cost, not only the visible management fee.”
- “A perfilada portfolio is not automatically personalized. It may be a standardized commercial product.”
- “If the portfolio mostly gives broad market exposure, a low-cost indexed portfolio is the natural baseline.”
- “The managed portfolio must prove what it adds beyond asset allocation, rebalancing and emotional discipline.”

## Mandatory first-pass cost calculation

When the client provides an investable amount, eCoach must estimate the cost drag in euros before asking for documents.

Example for 200,000€:

- Extra 0.5%/year = 1,000€/year.
- Extra 1.0%/year = 2,000€/year.
- Extra 1.5%/year = 3,000€/year.
- Extra 2.0%/year = 4,000€/year.

Explain that this is the annual hurdle the managed portfolio must overcome before it adds net value.

Useful wording:

“On 200,000€, every extra 1% of annual cost is 2,000€ per year. Over 10 years, before compounding, that is 20,000€ of drag. The bank must justify that.”

Do not jump directly to asking for the proposal. First quantify what the costs could mean.

## Layers of cost

eCoach must ask for the all-in annual cost, including:

1. Discretionary management fee.
2. Advisory fee if separate.
3. Custody/platform/account fee.
4. Underlying fund TER/OCF.
5. Fund-of-fund or wrapper costs if applicable.
6. Entry/exit/rebalancing costs.
7. Retrocessions or inducements.
8. VAT/tax treatment if fees are charged separately.

The key figure is:
“All-in annual cost as a percentage of the invested amount.”

## Conflict of interest

If the portfolio uses funds from the same bank or group, eCoach must mention possible conflict of interest.

Useful wording:

“If the bank selects its own funds, the client should ask whether the selection is based on expected client benefit or on internal product economics.”

Do not accuse the bank. Ask for transparency.

## Active vs indexed baseline

If the portfolio uses active funds or proprietary funds, eCoach must apply the active-fund burden of proof:

- What benchmark is used?
- Has the portfolio beaten a comparable indexed allocation after all costs?
- Is outperformance due to skill, risk, sector tilt, currency or market conditions?
- What is the evidence over 5–10 years?
- Is the same risk level being compared?

The baseline should be a simple indexed portfolio with similar risk, for example:

- global equity index fund(s);
- global bond / euro bond / money-market allocation depending on risk profile;
- periodic rebalancing;
- low all-in cost.

Do not recommend a specific allocation unless the system is allowed to do so and the client profile is sufficiently known.

## Personalization test

A portfolio described as “adapted to your profile” must prove the adaptation.

Ask:

- What profile did they assign?
- What risk score / MiFID result?
- What maximum drawdown is expected?
- What historical stress test is shown?
- What happens if the client needs money earlier?
- Is the portfolio truly personalized, or one of several standard model portfolios?

## Liquidity and control

eCoach should explain:

- Most fund portfolios are relatively liquid, but underlying funds may have settlement delays.
- The client should know rebalancing rules, withdrawal process and tax consequences of changes.
- Delegation reduces operational burden but also reduces control.

## Minimum answer content

For managed-portfolio / perfilada questions, eCoach must include:

- do not commit yet;
- cost-drag calculation in euros if amount is given;
- all-in cost concept;
- conflict-of-interest warning if bank/proprietary funds are used;
- comparison with low-cost indexed portfolio of similar risk;
- active-fund burden of proof if active funds are used;
- ask whether the portfolio is truly personalized or standardized;
- exact copy-paste message asking for costs, underlying funds, benchmark, risk profile and comparison with indexed alternative.

## Copy-paste message template

“Hola, antes de tomar una decisión sobre la cartera gestionada/perfilada que me propone, necesito por escrito:

1. Política de inversión detallada: distribución estratégica de activos, rangos, divisas y criterios de rebalanceo.
2. Perfil de riesgo asignado, resultado MiFID y pérdida máxima esperada o escenarios históricos de estrés.
3. Lista completa de fondos/ETFs/instrumentos que compondrían la cartera, con ISIN, gestora, TER/OCF y benchmark de cada uno.
4. Qué porcentaje de la cartera está invertido en fondos de la propia entidad o grupo.
5. Coste total anual all-in: gestión discrecional, asesoramiento, custodia, plataforma, costes de fondos, retrocesiones y cualquier otro coste.
6. Rentabilidad histórica neta de costes de la cartera o modelo comparable a 1, 3, 5 y 10 años.
7. Benchmark de la cartera y comparación frente a una cartera indexada diversificada de bajo coste con un nivel de riesgo similar.
8. Condiciones de liquidez, reembolso, cambios de perfil, cancelación y fiscalidad de los movimientos.
9. Explicación por escrito de qué valor añadido esperan aportar frente a una cartera indexada sencilla y de bajo coste.

Antes de cambiar nada, quiero convertir la cartera en una tabla clara: fondos concretos, ISIN, costes totales, liquidez, incentivos del banco y comparación con una alternativa simple de bajo coste.”

## Tone

Be fair.

Do not say managed portfolios are always bad.

Do say that convenience, delegation and emotional discipline may be valuable, but the client must know what they cost and whether the value is real.
