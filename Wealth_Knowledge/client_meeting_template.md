# Client Meeting Template

## Purpose

This file provides a practical structure for preparing and running a wealth-management client meeting.

The assistant helps prepare the conversation.
The assistant does not recommend investments.
The client or human advisor decides.

---

## 1. Meeting objective

Clarify:

- What is the purpose of this meeting?
- Is this a first discovery meeting?
- Is this a portfolio review?
- Is this a product discussion?
- Is this a platform/custody discussion?
- Is this a follow-up after receiving documents?
- What decision, if any, might be needed after the meeting?

---

## 2. Client context summary

Prepare a short summary:

- Age
- Residence
- Tax residence
- Family situation
- Dependants
- Employment or business situation
- Source of wealth
- Current assets
- Main concerns
- Main objective

## 4. Missing information

Identify what is still unknown:

- Income needs
- Full asset picture
- Debts or obligations
- Emergency fund
- Time horizon buckets
- Risk tolerance
- Capacity for loss
- Existing products
- Costs
- Tax details
- ESG preferences
- Documents not yet received

---

## 5. Questions to ask

Group questions by topic:

### Objective

- What is the money for?
- What would a successful outcome look like?
- Is the goal essential or flexible?

### Time horizon

- When will the money be needed?
- Is the capital needed all at once or gradually?
- Are there different time buckets?

### Liquidity

- How much cash must remain available?
- Are there expected expenses in the next 12, 24, or 60 months?

### Risk

- What temporary loss would be emotionally difficult?
- What temporary loss would create real-life financial problems?
- Has the client experienced market declines before?

### Knowledge and experience

- What products has the client used before?
- Does the client understand volatility, liquidity, fees, and currency risk?

### Tax and reporting

- What tax residence applies?
- Are there foreign accounts or assets?
- Should a tax advisor verify the structure?

---

## 6. Documents to request

Possible documents:

- Current portfolio statement
- Cash account statement
- Product names and ISINs
- Fund factsheets
- Cost breakdown
- Tax residence information
- Pension information
- Expected cash-flow needs
- Existing advisory agreements
- Business sale documentation if relevant
- Legal or inheritance documents if relevant

---

## 7. Risks to discuss

Potential risk areas:

- Market risk
- Inflation risk
- Liquidity risk
- Currency risk
- Concentration risk
- Interest-rate risk
- Credit risk
- Product complexity
- Tax/reporting risk
- Behavioural risk
- Sequence-of-returns risk
- Provider/platform risk

---

## 8. Options to compare

Only compare categories, not final recommendations.

Possible categories:

- Cash and deposits
- Treasury bills or short-term government debt
- Money market funds
- Bond funds
- Equity funds
- ETFs
- Indexed funds
- Robo-advisors
- Traditional banks
- Private banks
- Online brokers
- Fund platforms

---

## 9. Follow-up actions

After the meeting, define:

- What information is still missing?
- Who will provide which document?
- What analysis should be prepared next?
- What options need comparison?
- What risks need further explanation?
- What is the next small action?

---
