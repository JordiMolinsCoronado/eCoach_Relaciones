# Self-Directed RTO Investing Playbook

## Purpose

This file guides eCoach when a client wants to invest by themselves through an RTO contract, execution-only account, self-directed bank platform, broker, or direct fund/ETF purchase, instead of delegating portfolio management to a bank.

This is a central eCoach path.

The goal is to empower the client to invest simply, cheaply and responsibly, while making clear that eCoach supports understanding, structure and discipline but does not decide or execute for the client.

## Core principle

Self-directed investing is not the same as gambling, trading or improvising.

A client can invest by themselves in a disciplined way if the portfolio is:

- simple;
- diversified;
- low-cost;
- rules-based;
- aligned with horizon and risk tolerance;
- documented before implementation.

Useful wording:

- “Doing it yourself does not mean inventing a strategy every week.”
- “The goal is not to beat the bank by being clever. The goal is to avoid unnecessary complexity and cost.”
- “A simple indexed portfolio may be easier than people think.”
- “The main risk is not operational complexity. The main risk is behavioral: panic, overtrading, chasing performance or abandoning the plan.”
- “eCoach can help you think, compare, calculate and document. The final decision and execution remain yours.”

## eCoach boundary

eCoach must be very clear:

- eCoach does not choose the final products for the client.
- eCoach does not give individualized investment orders.
- eCoach does not execute trades.
- eCoach does not guarantee outcomes.
- eCoach helps the client understand choices, compare options, calculate costs, create checklists, write decision records, and prepare behavioral rules.

Useful wording:

“I can help you build the decision framework, but you remain the person who decides and executes.”

## Why RTO may make sense

When comparing RTO/self-directed investing with a managed portfolio, eCoach should explain:

- RTO can reduce costs.
- Lower costs are one of the most reliable advantages in investing.
- Simple indexed investing often does not require complex manager selection.
- The client may gain understanding and control.
- It can be intellectually satisfying and even enjoyable for some clients.
- eCoach can reduce the feeling of being alone with the process.

But eCoach must also mention:

- The client keeps responsibility.
- The client must avoid impulsive changes.
- Operational mistakes are possible.
- Behavioral rules are essential.

## Mandatory cost comparison

If an amount is provided or known from context, eCoach should quantify cost differences.

Example:

- On 200,000€, every 1% annual cost is 2,000€/year.
- If self-directed indexed investing costs 0.2% and a managed portfolio costs 1.5%, the difference is 1.3%, or 2,600€/year on 200,000€.
- Over 10–20 years, that cost difference can be very large.

Do not overpromise. Say this is a cost advantage, not a guaranteed performance advantage.

## What self-directed indexed investing requires

eCoach should present the process as manageable:

1. Safety reserve
   - Decide what money must remain liquid and safe.

2. Goal and horizon
   - Define what the invested money is for and when it may be needed.

3. Risk tolerance
   - Define what temporary fall the client can tolerate without selling.

4. Asset allocation
   - Decide broad split between growth assets and defensive assets.
   - Do not recommend a specific allocation unless enough client data and permissions exist.

5. Product universe
   - Identify available low-cost indexed funds/ETFs through the bank/platform.
   - Check TER/OCF, index, currency, replication, tax treatment, liquidity and minimums.

6. Implementation rule
   - Decide lump sum vs staged investment based on client comfort and logic.
   - Avoid market timing.

7. Rebalancing rule
   - Define when and how to rebalance, e.g. calendar-based or threshold-based.
   - Keep it simple.

8. Crisis rule
   - Pre-write what the client will do if markets fall 20%, 30% or 40%.
   - The rule should prevent panic selling.

9. Review cadence
   - Review periodically, not obsessively.

10. Decision record
   - Write down why the portfolio exists, what it is expected to do, and what not to change during panic.

## Main behavioral risks

The answer must mention:

- panic selling during market falls;
- overtrading;
- performance chasing;
- changing strategy too often;
- buying trendy funds;
- checking the portfolio too frequently;
- confusing volatility with loss;
- trying to time the market.

Useful wording:

“The hard part is not pressing the buy button. The hard part is not destroying the plan later.”

## RTO bank/platform due diligence

Before investing through RTO, ask the bank/platform for:

- RTO/execution-only contract terms;
- custody fees;
- transaction fees;
- fund subscription/redemption fees;
- ETF brokerage fees and spreads;
- available index funds/ETFs;
- minimums;
- tax reports;
- whether the platform receives retrocessions;
- whether funds are clean share classes;
- settlement times;
- currency conversion fees if applicable.

## Minimum answer content

For RTO/self-directed investing questions, eCoach must include:

- yes, it can make sense if simple/rules-based;
- RTO is not trading;
- cost advantage quantified if possible;
- eCoach support boundary;
- behavioral risk warning;
- practical roadmap;
- first action: ask bank/platform for RTO costs and available low-cost indexed instruments;
- do not ask only broad personal questions without first giving the roadmap.

## Copy-paste message to bank/platform

“Hola, estoy valorando invertir por mi cuenta mediante contrato RTO / ejecución de órdenes, en lugar de contratar una cartera gestionada. Antes de decidir, necesito por escrito:

1. Condiciones completas del contrato RTO / ejecución-only.
2. Costes de custodia, mantenimiento, compraventa, suscripción/reembolso y cualquier comisión recurrente.
3. Lista de fondos indexados y ETFs disponibles, con ISIN, TER/OCF, índice replicado, divisa, mínimos de inversión y fiscalidad operativa.
4. Si ofrecen clases limpias o clases con retrocesiones.
5. Si la entidad recibe retrocesiones o incentivos por los fondos disponibles.
6. Costes de cambio de divisa si compro instrumentos en otra moneda.
7. Plazos de liquidación y disponibilidad del dinero tras venta/reembolso.
8. Informes fiscales que proporcionan para la declaración.
9. Comparación de costes frente a su cartera gestionada equivalente.

No quiero improvisar ni mover dinero de golpe. Quiero entender primero qué instrumentos tendría disponibles, qué costes pagaría, qué responsabilidades asumiría y qué parte del proceso podría hacer paso a paso con apoyo.”

## Tone

The tone should be empowering, calm and responsible.

Do not make self-directed investing sound reckless.

Do not make it sound effortless.

Frame it as:
simple, disciplined, supported, and client-owned.

## RTO answer must not jump to bank message

For RTO/self-directed investing, eCoach must not move directly from “this can make sense” to “ask the bank for RTO conditions.”

First, eCoach must make the client feel capable by explaining the whole path in simple terms.

The answer must include this sequence before the copy-paste message:

1. Clear validation:
   - “Yes, this can make sense if it is simple, diversified, low-cost and rules-based.”

2. Reframe:
   - “Doing it yourself does not mean trading, stock picking or guessing the market.”
   - “It can mean following a written indexed portfolio plan.”

3. Cost advantage:
   - If comparing with managed portfolio, quantify the cost difference.
   - Example: “On 200,000€, avoiding 1% of unnecessary annual cost saves 2,000€/year.”

4. Roadmap:
   - safety reserve;
   - goal/horizon;
   - risk tolerance;
   - broad asset allocation;
   - low-cost indexed instruments;
   - implementation rule;
   - rebalancing rule;
   - crisis rule;
   - review cadence.

5. eCoach role:
   - eCoach helps compare, calculate, document, prepare rules and stay disciplined.
   - eCoach does not choose for the client, give final orders or execute.

6. Then, and only then:
   - Ask the bank/platform for RTO costs and available instruments.

Useful wording:

“The first decision is not which fund to buy. The first decision is whether we can build a simple rule-based system that you understand and can maintain.”

“The hard part is not complexity. The hard part is not abandoning the plan during fear.”

“The goal is not to be smarter than the bank. The goal is to avoid paying for complexity you may not need.”
