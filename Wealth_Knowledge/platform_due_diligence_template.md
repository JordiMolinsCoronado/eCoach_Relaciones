# Platform Due Diligence Template

## Purpose

This file helps compare platform, custody, broker, bank, and fund-platform options.

The assistant may compare categories and criteria.
The assistant must not recommend one platform as the final best choice.

---

## 1. Platform category

Identify the type of platform:

- Traditional bank
- Private bank
- Online broker
- Fund platform
- Robo-advisor
- Indexed fund platform
- Money market / cash account route
- Treasury bills / short-term government debt route
- Insurance wrapper or pension route
- Other

---

## 2. Client operational need

Clarify what the client needs:

- Simple custody
- Simple tax reporting
- Low costs
- Access to indexed funds
- Access to ETFs
- Access to clean share classes
- Discretionary management
- Advisor-assisted management
- Self-directed investing
- Income withdrawals
- Multi-currency handling
- Consolidated reporting
- Low administrative burden

---

## 3. Regulation and investor protection

Check:

- Is the platform regulated?
- In which country?
- Which regulator supervises it?
- What investor-protection scheme applies?
- What deposit guarantee scheme applies, if any?
- Are assets segregated from the platformâ€™s balance sheet?
- What happens if the platform fails?

---

## 4. Costs

Ask for a full cost breakdown:

- Custody fee
- Account maintenance fee
- Trading fee
- ETF transaction cost
- Fund subscription fee
- Fund redemption fee
- Currency conversion fee
- Transfer fee
- Advisory fee
- Discretionary-management fee
- Fund TER / OCF
- Retrocessions
- Availability of clean share classes
- Hidden spreads or execution costs

---

## 5. Product availability

Clarify whether the platform gives access to:

- Deposits
- Treasury bills
- Money market funds
- Bond funds
- Equity funds
- Indexed funds
- ETFs
- Clean share classes
- Spanish funds
- Luxembourg/Ireland funds
- Pension products
- Structured products
- Private markets
- Foreign-currency assets

---

## 6. Tax and reporting convenience

Check:

- Does the platform provide Spanish tax reporting?
- Does it provide annual tax statements?
- Does it simplify capital gains reporting?
- Does it report dividends and interest clearly?
- Does it handle withholding tax information?
- Are foreign-account reporting obligations relevant?
- Could Modelo 720 or other reporting obligations arise?
- Should a tax advisor verify the reporting implications?

---

## 7. Operational friction

Assess:

- Account opening difficulty
- Transfer-in process
- Transfer-out process
- Time to move funds
- Client interface quality
- Mobile/web usability
- Customer service quality
- Language support
- Advisor access
- Documentation quality
- Ease of withdrawals
- Estate/inheritance procedures

---

## 8. Execution and custody quality

Ask:

- How are orders executed?
- Is execution direct or via another intermediary?
- Are there execution-quality reports?
- Are assets held in nominee accounts?
- Are assets individually registered?
- Are there sub-custodians?
- Is there securities lending?
- Are there restrictions on transfers?

---

## 9. Risks and warnings

Flag possible issues:

- High total cost
- Poor tax reporting
- Limited product universe
- Lack of clean share classes
- Complex custody chain
- Weak customer service
- Transfer-out friction
- Currency-conversion costs
- Product pushing
- Conflicts of interest
- Over-reliance on one provider
- Platform outside clientâ€™s tax/reporting comfort zone

---

## 10. Questions before choosing

Ask:

- What problem is this platform meant to solve?
- What alternatives are being compared?
- What are the all-in costs?
- What products are actually needed?
- How important is Spanish tax-reporting convenience?
- Does the client want self-directed or advised management?
- How easily can assets be transferred out?
- What happens if the platform changes fees?
- What happens if the platform fails?
- Who verifies tax and legal implications?
