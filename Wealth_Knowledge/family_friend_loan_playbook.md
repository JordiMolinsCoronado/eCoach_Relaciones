# Family / friend loan playbook

## Scope

Use this playbook when the client is asked to lend money to:
- family member;
- friend;
- romantic partner;
- sibling;
- parent/child;
- someone close with emotional pressure.

The client should not lend money under guilt, urgency, shame or emotional pressure.

## Gift / loan / investment distinction

Force the distinction:

1. Gift:
   - only if the client can afford never to recover the money;
   - emotionally cleaner;
   - should be limited.

2. Loan:
   - must have written terms;
   - repayment calendar;
   - what happens if payments fail;
   - whether interest applies;
   - whether there are guarantees.

3. Investment in business:
   - much riskier;
   - needs business numbers;
   - may be lost entirely;
   - should not be disguised as a family favor.

Do not allow the client to call something a "loan" if there is no contract, no repayment calendar and no consequence for default.

## Client priority

The client's emergency fund, mortgage, children, taxes and core obligations come before rescuing another adult's business.

This does not mean being selfish.
It means not endangering one household to rescue another.

## Safer alternatives

Suggest alternatives:
- smaller capped amount treated as possible gift;
- help review business numbers;
- help negotiate with creditors;
- help find accountant/lawyer;
- introduce potential clients;
- split aid into monthly limited support;
- only release money in tranches after written milestones;
- say no kindly.

## Business rescue warning

If money is for "saving a business", ask:
- what exactly caused the cash need;
- current debt;
- revenue and margins;
- monthly cash burn;
- what changes after receiving the money;
- why bank/partners/investors are not funding it;
- repayment source;
- worst-case scenario.

If the business cannot show how the loan is repaid, the money may be a rescue gift, not a loan.

## Copy-paste message

Provide a message that is warm but bounded.

It should say:
- I want to help if I can;
- I cannot decide from guilt or verbally;
- I need written repayment plan;
- if it is a loan, it needs written terms;
- I must protect my household first;
- once I see the plan, I will decide what amount, if any, I can afford.

## Answer structure

1. Acknowledge emotional difficulty.
2. Do not transfer money yet.
3. Calculate concentration.
4. Ask: can you afford to lose it?
5. Distinguish gift / loan / investment.
6. Explain no contract = red flag.
7. Protect client's family obligations.
8. Suggest smaller/safer help.
9. Provide copy-paste message.
10. One next action.
