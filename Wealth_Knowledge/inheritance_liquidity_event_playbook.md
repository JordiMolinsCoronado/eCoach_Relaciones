## Decision buckets

A good answer should organize the money into buckets:

1. Taxes and legal costs:
   - inheritance tax;
   - municipal capital gains tax if real estate inherited/sold;
   - legal/notary/registry costs;
   - reserve until clear.

2. Emotional pause:
   - no irreversible decisions for a short period if client is emotionally affected;
   - money can be parked safely while deciding.

3. Emergency fund:
   - keep enough liquid cash for household stability.

4. Short-term parking:
   - deposits, remunerated accounts, Treasury bills, money market funds;
   - safety/liquidity while deciding.

5. Mortgage decision:
   - partial or full amortization;
   - reduce term vs reduce payment;
   - guaranteed saving equal to mortgage cost;
   - loss of liquidity.

6. Long-term diversified investing:
   - phased investment if client fears entering all at once;
   - low-cost diversified baseline.

7. Real estate purchase:
   - only after full rental calculation;
   - do not buy property simply because cash is available;
   - check taxes, expenses, vacancy, repairs, illiquidity, concentration and regulation.

## Mortgage comparison

If mortgage rate is given:
- explain amortization as known saving;
- compare against uncertain investment returns after taxes/costs;
- warn that full cancellation may reduce liquidity too much.

If mortgage rate is 3.1%:
"Amortizar gives a known saving around 3.1% before considering taxes/deductions and commissions. Investing may earn more, but with uncertainty."

## Real estate warning

When the client considers buying a rental property:
- do not dismiss it automatically;
- insist on full net calculation before purchase;
- warn about concentration, illiquidity, tenant risk, repairs and taxes;
- compare against diversified index funds.

Useful wording:
"Comprar un piso para alquilar no es la default answer to having cash. It is a leveraged, concentrated, illiquid business decision."

## Indexed funds

If the client already has funds:
- acknowledge existing investment experience;
- compare the new amount to existing invested amount;
- suggest using an investment policy / phased implementation if fear is high;
- do not recommend specific funds.

## Copy-paste messages

Depending on case, provide one message only, choosing the most urgent bottleneck.

Usually first bottleneck:
- tax/legal status if inheritance tax unknown; or
- mortgage amortization simulation if taxes are already settled.

If many unknowns:
next action should be to list the current facts and reserve taxes, not ask five institutions at once.

## Answer structure

1. Acknowledge emotional weight.
2. No rush / no irreversible decision yet.
3. First-pass calculations.
4. Bucket the money.
5. Compare main options:
   - mortgage;
   - funds;
   - real estate;
   - deposits/cash parking.
6. Identify the first bottleneck.
7. Provide one copy-paste message if useful.
8. One next action.

## Inheritance tax wording precision

Do not say generically that "a large/important part" of the inheritance may go to Hacienda.

Inheritance tax varies greatly by autonomous community, relationship, pre-existing wealth, deductions and whether the inheritance is cash, property or other assets.
