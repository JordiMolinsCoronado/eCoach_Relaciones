# Funds of Funds Playbook

## Purpose

This file guides eCoach when a client asks about fund of funds, fondo de fondos, multi-manager funds, fund selection products, delegated fund selection, funds that invest in other funds, or portfolios packaged as a single fund of funds.

The goal is to reveal cost layers, overlap, active manager-selection risk and the burden of proof versus a simple low-cost indexed portfolio.

## Core principle

A fund of funds is not automatically better diversified because it owns many funds.

It may add:

- diversification across managers or strategies;
- convenience;
- professional selection.

But it may also add:

- a second layer of fees;
- hidden underlying fund costs;
- overlap between underlying funds;
- manager-selection risk;
- less transparency;
- conflicts of interest if underlying funds belong to the same bank/group.

Useful wording:

- “Many funds does not automatically mean better diversification.”
- “A fund of funds can diversify managers, but it can also duplicate the same exposures.”
- “The key figure is the total all-in cost, including the underlying funds.”
- “The product must prove what it adds beyond a simple diversified indexed portfolio.”

## Mandatory first-pass calculation

When the client gives an amount, eCoach must quantify cost drag.

Example for 150,000€:

- Extra 0.5%/year = 750€/year.
- Extra 1.0%/year = 1,500€/year.
- Extra 1.5%/year = 2,250€/year.
- Extra 2.0%/year = 3,000€/year.

Explain that this is the annual hurdle the product must overcome.

## Cost layers

eCoach must ask for:

1. Fund-of-funds management fee.
2. Depositary/admin fee.
3. Underlying fund TER/OCF.
4. Performance fees if any.
5. Entry/exit fees.
6. Custody/platform fees.
7. Retrocessions/inducements.
8. Any VAT/tax treatment of advisory fees if relevant.

The key question:

“What is the total annual all-in cost paid directly or indirectly by the client?”

## Overlap risk

eCoach must explain that multiple underlying funds may hold the same securities.

Example:

A fund of funds may own 15 global equity funds, but many of them may all hold Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, Nestlé, ASML or other large-cap names. The client may think they own 15 different strategies while economically owning overlapping market exposure with higher costs.

Ask for:

- top 10 underlying funds;
- top holdings look-through if available;
- regional/sector exposures;
- active vs passive split;
- whether overlap is measured.

## Manager-selection risk

A fund of funds adds a manager-selection layer.

The client is not only betting on markets. They are also betting that the fund-of-funds manager can select and replace underlying managers well enough to overcome the extra cost.

Ask:

- selection criteria;
- replacement criteria;
- historical evidence;
- whether past outperformance came from manager skill, market beta, style tilt or luck.

## Conflict of interest

If the underlying funds are from the bank/group, eCoach must mention possible conflict of interest.

Ask what percentage is invested in proprietary funds and whether cheaper external or indexed alternatives were considered.

## Comparison baseline

The baseline is a simple diversified low-cost indexed portfolio with similar risk.

The fund of funds must prove:

- better diversification after overlap;
- better risk-adjusted net performance after all costs;
- useful access that the client cannot obtain otherwise;
- behavioral/convenience value worth the cost.

## Minimum answer content

For fund-of-funds questions, eCoach must include:

- do not sign yet;
- two-layer cost explanation;
- euro cost-drag calculation if amount is given;
- overlap warning;
- manager-selection risk;
- conflict-of-interest warning if bank/proprietary funds are possible;
- comparison with low-cost indexed portfolio;
- exact copy-paste message asking for underlying funds, ISINs, costs, overlap, benchmarks, active/passive split, retrocessions and liquidity.

## Copy-paste message template

“Hola, antes de tomar una decisión sobre el fondo de fondos que me propone, necesito por escrito:

1. Nombre completo, ISIN y KID/DFI del fondo de fondos.
2. Lista completa de fondos subyacentes, con ISIN, peso, gestora, TER/OCF, benchmark y si pertenecen al grupo del banco.
3. Coste total anual all-in, incluyendo comisión del fondo de fondos, depositaría, costes de fondos subyacentes, retrocesiones, comisiones de éxito y cualquier otro coste directo o indirecto.
4. Porcentaje de fondos activos, indexados y fondos de la propia entidad/grupo.
5. Análisis de solapamiento: principales posiciones/look-through, exposición por regiones, sectores y divisas.
6. Criterios de selección y sustitución de fondos subyacentes.
7. Rentabilidad histórica neta a 1, 3, 5 y 10 años frente a benchmark y frente a una cartera indexada diversificada de bajo coste y riesgo similar.
8. Condiciones de liquidez, reembolso, ventanas, penalizaciones y fiscalidad operativa.
9. Explicación escrita de qué valor añadido aporta este fondo de fondos frente a una cartera indexada sencilla y de bajo coste.

Antes de cambiar nada, quiero entender el coste total, el solapamiento real entre fondos y la comparación con una alternativa diversificada más simple y de bajo coste.”

## Tone

Be fair.

Do not say funds of funds are always bad.

Do say that “more layers” must prove “more value,” not just more complexity and more cost.
