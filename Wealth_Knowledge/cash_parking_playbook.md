# Cash Parking Playbook

## Purpose

This file guides eCoach when a client asks where to park cash safely or compares deposits, remunerated accounts, money-market funds, Treasury bills, short-term bond funds, or similar low-risk liquidity products.

The goal is to help the client compare safety, liquidity, yield, tax, guarantee and access to money without treating all “low-risk” products as the same.

## Core principle

For cash parking, the main question is not maximum return.

The main question is:

- when the client may need the money;
- what guarantee/protection exists;
- how quickly the money can be accessed;
- what the net return is after costs and tax;
- what risk is being accepted for extra yield.

Useful wording:

- “Safety is not one single thing: legal guarantee, market risk, issuer risk and liquidity are different.”
- “A money-market fund is low risk, but it is not a deposit.”
- “A deposit may be guaranteed, but it may not be liquid before maturity.”
- “The right comparison is net return, access time and protection, not just headline rate.”

## Mandatory first-pass calculation

When amount and rate are provided, eCoach must calculate the gross return.

Example:

100,000€ at 2.5% for 12 months = 2,500€ gross interest.

If relevant, estimate after-tax return in simple terms:

If taxed at 19%, 2,500€ gross becomes about 2,025€ net.

Use cautious wording because tax rates may vary by country, year and total savings income.

Do not ask for personal details before doing the calculation that can already be done.

## Deposits and remunerated accounts

Explain:

- Deposits and bank accounts may be covered by the deposit guarantee scheme up to the applicable legal limit per depositor and entity, if eligible.
- The client must confirm the entity, country and guarantee scheme.
- If the client places exactly the guarantee limit, accrued interest may exceed the protected amount unless handled separately.
- Deposits may have early-cancellation restrictions or loss of interest.
- Accounts usually have more liquidity but the rate may be variable and subject to conditions.

Ask for:

- TAE/APR;
- nominal rate;
- term;
- whether rate is fixed or variable;
- cancellation rights;
- penalty/loss of interest;
- maximum remunerated balance;
- linked-product requirements;
- guarantee scheme and entity.

## Money-market funds

Explain:

- Money-market funds are investment funds, not deposits.
- They are not covered by the deposit guarantee scheme.
- They are usually low-risk and liquid, but not risk-free.
- NAV can fluctuate slightly.
- Returns vary with short-term interest rates.
- Liquidity is usually daily but settlement can take one or more business days.
- TER/OCF and platform/custody fees reduce net return.

Ask for:

- fund ISIN;
- type of money-market fund;
- TER/OCF;
- underlying assets;
- average maturity/duration if available;
- credit quality;
- currency;
- settlement time;
- minimum investment;
- custody/platform fees.

## Treasury bills / government bills

If relevant, explain:

- Treasury bills may be high-quality short-term government debt.
- They are not the same as a bank deposit.
- Price, yield, maturity and custody/transaction costs matter.
- Liquidity before maturity may involve price movement.

## Comparison table requirement

For cash-parking answers, eCoach should include a simple comparison:

- Deposit: guarantee, fixed return, less liquidity.
- Remunerated account: high liquidity, rate may change, conditions.
- Money-market fund: no FGD, low market risk, usually liquid, variable return, fees.
- Treasury bills if relevant: government credit risk, maturity, market price if sold early.

## Minimum answer content

For cash parking / deposit vs money market questions, eCoach must include:

- gross interest calculation if amount/rate given;
- net-after-tax reminder;
- FGD/guarantee distinction;
- liquidity/access-time distinction;
- money-market fund not a deposit;
- fees/TER/custody;
- TAE vs marketing rate;
- early cancellation conditions;
- comparison against the client’s time horizon and emergency-liquidity needs;
- exact copy-paste message to bank/platform asking for details.

## Copy-paste message template

“Hola, antes de decidir dónde colocar este dinero, necesito por escrito:

1. Para el depósito/cuenta: TAE, tipo nominal, plazo, importe máximo remunerado y si la remuneración es fija o variable.
2. Condiciones de cancelación anticipada: si puedo retirar antes, pérdida de intereses, penalizaciones y plazo de disponibilidad.
3. Confirmación del Fondo de Garantía de Depósitos aplicable: entidad, país, límite por titular y entidad, y si el producto está cubierto.
4. Si hay vinculaciones: nómina, tarjetas, seguros, permanencia u otros productos.
5. Para el fondo monetario: nombre, ISIN, KID/DFI, TER/OCF, divisa, activos subyacentes, calidad crediticia, duración/vencimiento medio, liquidez y plazo de liquidación.
6. Costes de plataforma, custodia, suscripción, reembolso o cualquier otro coste.
7. Rentabilidad estimada neta de costes y explicación de los riesgos principales.
8. Comparación clara entre depósito/cuenta y fondo monetario: garantía, liquidez, rentabilidad neta, fiscalidad y acceso al dinero.

Antes de elegir una opción, quiero entender la protección, la liquidez y la rentabilidad neta de cada alternativa.”

## Tone

Be calm and practical.

This is a low-risk category, so do not overdramatize.

But do not let the client confuse:
- deposit guarantee with fund liquidity;
- headline rate with net return;
- low risk with zero risk.
