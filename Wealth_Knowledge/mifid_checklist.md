# MiFID-Style Client Checklist

## Purpose

This file provides a structured checklist inspired by MiFID / EAF-style suitability conversations.

It is not a formal legal suitability assessment.
It is a preparation tool for gathering information before any human decision.

## 2. Investment objective

Clarify whether the main objective is:
- Capital preservation
- Retirement income
- Long-term growth
- Regular income
- Future real-estate purchase
- Education funding
- Business liquidity
- Inheritance planning
- Other specific goal

Ask:
- What is the money for?
- When will the money be needed?
- Is the objective essential or optional?
- Is the objective fixed or flexible?

## 4. Liquidity needs

Ask:
- How much cash must remain immediately available?
- What expenses are expected in the next 12 months?
- What expenses are expected in the next 24 months?
- What expenses are expected in the next 5 years?
- Is there an emergency fund outside the investment portfolio?

## 6. Capacity for loss

Ask:
- How much could the client lose without damaging real-life plans?
- What level of temporary drawdown would become a practical problem?
- Is there capital that must not be exposed to market risk?
- Would a 10%, 20%, or 30% decline create financial stress?
- Is the investment objective flexible if markets fall?

## 8. Knowledge and experience

## 9. Existing portfolio

Ask:
- What assets does the client currently hold?
- What are the weights?
- What are the ISINs or product names?
- What platforms or banks are used?
- What are the costs?
- What currency exposure exists?
- What unrealized gains or losses exist?
- Are there concentrated positions?

## 11. ESG or sustainability preferences

Ask:
- Does the client have ESG or sustainability preferences?
- Are there sectors the client wants to avoid?
- Does the client prefer impact-oriented investments?
- Are ESG preferences strict requirements or soft preferences?

## 12. Product restrictions

Clarify:
- Is the client willing to use ETFs?
- Is the client willing to use funds?
- Is the client willing to use illiquid products?
- Is the client willing to hold foreign-currency exposure?
- Does the client need simple tax reporting?
- Does the client need low-cost indexed products?

## 13. Documents needed

Request:
- Current portfolio statement
- Cash position statement
- Fund or ETF names and ISINs
- Cost breakdown
- Tax residence information
- Investment history
- Pension information
- Expected cash-flow needs
- Existing advisory agreements
- Relevant legal or inheritance documents

## 14. Red flags

Flag:
- Stated risk tolerance inconsistent with experience
- Short time horizon with high-risk assets
- Need for liquidity but use of illiquid products
- High concentration in one asset or provider
- Unknown costs
- Unknown tax situation
- Client wants return without volatility
- Client may panic sell during drawdowns
- Product complexity exceeds client knowledge

## Default reminder

This checklist is not an investment recommendation.
It is a preparation tool for human review and decision-making.
