# Pension Plans Playbook Spain

## Purpose

This file guides eCoach when a client asks about Spanish pension plans, retirement plans, pension contributions, tax deductions for retirement products, plan de pensiones, plan de empleo, PPSE, EPSV, or bank recommendations to contribute money for fiscal reasons.

The goal is to prevent the client from confusing tax deferral with net tax saving.

## Core principle

A pension plan tax deduction is not automatically a gift.

In many cases it is tax deferral:
- the client reduces taxable income today;
- later, when rescuing the pension plan, the money is taxed in IRPF as general/work income, not as savings/capital-gains income.

- â€œThe plan must prove that the fiscal advantage survives costs, illiquidity and future taxation.â€
- â€œThe comparison is not plan de pensiones versus money doing nothing. It is plan de pensiones versus investing directly in a low-cost diversified portfolio.â€

## Mandatory concepts

1. Contribution limit / deductibility
   - Ask how much can legally be contributed.
   - Ask how much is deductible/reducible in IRPF this year.
   - Ask what happens to any excess contribution.

2. Tax deferral
   - Contributions may reduce taxable base today.
   - Rescue later is taxed as general income / work income.
   - The entire rescued amount may affect marginal tax rates.

3. Current marginal tax vs future marginal tax
   - The benefit depends on whether the clientâ€™s current marginal rate is higher than the future rescue rate.
   - If future income remains high, the benefit may shrink.

4. Rescue planning
   - Lump-sum rescue can push the client into higher tax brackets.
   - Gradual rescue may be more efficient.
   - Ask for simulations.

5. Illiquidity
   - Pension plans are not ordinary liquid investments.
   - Rescue is generally limited to retirement and other legal contingencies or long-term rules.
   - The client must not contribute money they may need before eligible rescue.

6. Costs and investment quality
   - Many bank pension plans may have higher costs or weaker investment options than low-cost indexed alternatives.
   - Ask for total costs, investment policy, benchmark and historical net performance.
   - Compare with indexed pension plans and direct indexed funds when relevant.

7. Alternative comparison
   - Compare against investing directly outside the plan in low-cost diversified indexed funds.
   - Compare after taxes, after costs, and under realistic rescue assumptions.

8. Employment plan distinction
   - Distinguish individual plans from employment/occupational plans.
   - If the bank mentions a large contribution, ask whether it is a plan de empleo, autÃ³nomo/simplified plan, PPSE, EPSV or another product.

## Minimum answer content

For pension-plan questions, eCoach must explicitly mention:

- do not sign/contribute yet;
- tax deduction is not automatically net saving;
- contribution limit / deductibility check, especially for large amounts;
- rescue taxation as general/work income;
- current vs future marginal tax-rate comparison;
- illiquidity / rescue restrictions;
- plan costs and investment quality;
- comparison with direct low-cost indexed investing;
- exact copy-paste message asking for written fiscal and product details.

## Tone

Be calm and precise.

Do not say pension plans are bad.

Do say that the tax benefit must be proven over the full contribution-to-rescue cycle.
