# Startup / friend investment playbook

## Scope

Use this playbook when the client considers investing money in:
- a friend's startup;
- a family member's startup;
- an early-stage company;
- a business with no profits;
- an informal equity offer;
- a company where valuation, cap table or shareholder agreement is unclear.

## Core principle

A startup investment is high risk and can go to zero.

When the founder is a friend, there are two risks:
- financial loss;
- relationship damage.

Do not let friendship, excitement or FOMO replace due diligence.

## Distinguish categories

Force the distinction:

1. Gift/favor:
   - emotionally motivated;
   - should be small enough to lose;
   - no expectation of return.

2. Loan:
   - needs repayment schedule;
   - contract;
   - default consequences;
   - collateral/guarantee if relevant.

3. Equity investment:
   - needs valuation;
   - cap table;
   - shareholder agreement;
   - investor rights;
   - dilution terms;
   - exit logic.

4. Advisory/sweat contribution:
   - if the client contributes time/contact/expertise, compensation should be separate from cash investment.

## Required due diligence

Before investing, ask for:

- valuation: pre-money, post-money, how calculated;
- cap table before and after investment;
- percentage acquired and share class;
- shareholder agreement / pacto de socios;
- founder vesting or lockup;
- liquidation preference or preferential rights;
- anti-dilution or dilution expectations;
- future financing needs;
- runway and burn rate;
- current cash;
- revenue, margins, customers, pipeline;
- debt and liabilities;
- IP ownership;
- employment/contractor status of key people;
- use of funds;
- monthly reporting rights;
- exit scenarios;
- what happens if company fails or founder leaves.

## Ticket sizing

For highly risky private investments, suggest considering:
- a smaller ticket;
- staged investment/tranches;
- only money the client can afford to lose;
- no investment until emergency fund and core obligations are safe.

Do not recommend a specific amount unless framed as a risk-control example.

## Friend risk

A friend may be honest and still fail.

Useful wording:
"The question is not whether your friend is good. The question is whether the deal would still make sense if he were not your friend."

## Copy-paste message

Provide a warm but serious message asking for the full documentation.

The message should:
- show openness;
- say the client cannot decide on verbal promises;
- ask for valuation, cap table, pacto de socios, runway, burn, debt, IP, use of funds;
- ask what rights the investor receives;
- ask what happens in downside scenarios;
- say the client will review before deciding.

## Answer structure

1. Acknowledge excitement and relational pressure.
2. Do not transfer money yet.
3. Calculate concentration and implied valuation if possible.
4. Say startup investment can go to zero.
5. Distinguish favor/loan/equity/advisory.
6. Explain missing documents and rights.
7. Suggest smaller/staged ticket if appropriate.
8. Provide copy-paste message.
9. One next action.

## Quality floor

Must include:
- concentration calculation if savings and amount are given;
- implied valuation if amount and % are given;
- "can go to zero";
- cap table;
- pacto de socios;
- dilution/future rounds;
- runway/burn;
- IP/debt;
- written terms before money.
